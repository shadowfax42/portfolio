From Morocco to Silicon Valley

I came to the U.S. alone from Morocco at 19 years old with $100 in my pocket. I supported myself by working 5 a.m. shifts bussing tables, cleaning hotel rooms, and hosting at restaurants. I went to college at night and on my days off.

It was hard, but I learned how to stay focused and keep going, even when things were overwhelming. That experience shaped who I am today.

I was fortunate to attend Johns Hopkins University on a grant, earning my B.S. in Electrical Engineering and M.S.E in Electrical and Computer Engineering.

Today, I apply that same grit to solving complex data challenges. Whether I'm leading validation teams in the semiconductor industry or mentoring graduate students at Georgia Tech, I am driven by a commitment to technical excellence and a desire to empower the next generation of engineers.

Professionally, I specialize in Data Science and Machine Learning to solve complex problems. My background spans Full-Stack development and end-to-end analytics. Currently a Senior Data Scientist at Intel and an Instructional Associate at Georgia Tech, I combine technical leadership with a passion for teaching.

MIT Policy Hackathon 2025

Project Overview

This project addresses the 'Rider E' charge in Bowling Green, Ohio, a policy that currently penalizes residential solar adopters and limits the viability of Distributed Energy Resources (DERs). In response, we propose a Fair Energy Pricing Framework. This model balances the utility's need for cost recovery with fair compensation for the grid services provided by solar-plus-battery systems, specifically regarding grid stability and peak shaving.

Key Innovations:

  • ‚Ä¢ Data-driven solar deployment optimization
  • ‚Ä¢ Policy impact modeling and analysis
